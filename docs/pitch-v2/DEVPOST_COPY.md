## One-Liner

VibeShield is a live risk debugger for AI products: it tests one deployed feature, captures the exact tool-boundary failure, applies a guard, and reruns the same attack to prove the fix.

## Short Description

Vibe coding makes it easy to ship an AI app that appears to work. VibeShield focuses on the next problem: what happens when untrusted users talk to an agent that can refund customers, access PII, or call external tools.

Our hackathon demo uses a controlled AI customer-support/refund flow with fake customer data and fake external tools. VibeShield maps the risky workflow, runs a bounded attack, captures evidence, applies a plan-aware runtime guard, and verifies that the attack is stopped while a normal support task still works.

Legal and platform items are shown only as risk indicators for developer or legal review, not legal advice.

## What We Built

- A demo risk loop for a support/refund AI agent.
- Static tool-risk mapping for high-impact tools such as refunds, external email, plan updates, and PII lookup.
- Runtime evidence flow for before/after behavior when daemon artifacts are available.
- Guard framing that requires approval or blocking for high-impact actions outside the trusted plan.
- A pitch and judge Q&A package that separates live proof from roadmap readiness checks.

## How It Works

1. Pick one risky workflow, such as Support / Refund Flow.
2. Run a bounded assessment against that authorized flow.
3. Open the critical finding and trace.
4. Apply a guard or policy.
5. Rerun the same attack plus a mutation.
6. Confirm benign utility still passes.
7. Keep the check in CI or as a recorded regression artifact.
